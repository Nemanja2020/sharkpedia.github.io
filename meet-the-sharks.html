SHARKS

GREAt WHITE SHARK

Where they live?:

Great white sharks can be found in every ocean, but they stay away from the colder waters of Antarctica and the Arctic.

What do they eat?:

fish, invertebrates, and marine mammals.

Fun Facts:

-Great White Sharks existed before dinosaurs

-Great Whites have social hierarchy

-The Great Whites biggest threat is human interaction

WHALE SHARK

Where they live?:

tropical and warm temperate seas, usually between latitudes 30°N and 35°S.

What do they eat?:

plankton or fish.

Fun Facts:

-Whale sharks have teeth on their eyes

-Whale sharks are ovoviviparous

-Whale sharks are filter feeders

LEMON SHARK

Where they live?:

Lemon sharks inhabit coastal inshore waters from New Jersey (US) to Southern Brazil, Gulf of Mexico and Caribbean in the northwestern Atlantic Ocean.

What do they eat?:

bony fish and crustaceans

Fun Facts:

-Lemon sharks are named for their yellow-brown skin that helps them camouflage in the sandy, tropical waters they inhabit.

-They have friends! Research shows that they make preferential social connections with other lemons sharks.

-Lemon Sharks can get jealous and possessive,if they see their favorite human with another shark they will chase that shark away.

HAMMERHEAD

Where they live?:

temperate and tropical waters worldwide.

What do they eat?:

stingrays, other rays and skates.

Fun Facts:

-Hammerheads have eyes that allow 360 degrees of vision but cannot see in front of them

-Great hammerhead sharks can swim sideways

-Hammerheads love to travel in groups

TIGER SHARK

Where they live?:

tropical and subtropical areas.

What do they eat?:

The tiger shark is a non-selective feeder and a voracious predator. It has been known to consume a wide variety of marine prey and even some terrestrial animals.

Fun Facts:

- Tiger sharks are named for the distinctive, gray vertical stripes or spots covering the sides of their bodies.

-Female tiger sharks have anywhere from 10 to 82 embryos and give birth to an average of 30 to 35 pups per litter.

-Tiger sharks can grow up to 18 feet (5.5 m) long and 2,000 pounds (900 kg), making it one of the largest shark species.

BULL SHARK

Where they live?:

coastal waters all over the world.

What do they eat?:

almost anything, but their diet consists mainly of fish. They also sometimes eat dolphins and sea turtles. Bull sharks even eat other sharks.

Fun Facts:

-They Can Thrive in Freshwater or Saltwater

-They Can Swim Surprisingly Far Upriver

-They Are Much Less Dangerous to Us Than We Are to Them
